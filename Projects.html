HEAT TRANSFER RATE ENHANCEMENT OF AN ANTI-FOULING LAYER COATED HEAT EXCHANGER BASED CHILLER WITH A NOVEL IONIC NANOFLUID
HEAT EXCHANGER

February 2019

Absorption chillers powered by Gas Turbine exhaust gases utilizes waste heat for power generation in the oil and gas industry. On the grounds, absorption chillers are important components for polygeneration, which can improve plant utilization and efficiency of the overall energy supply system. Poor heat transfer performance and fouling in the heat exchanger of chillers are the ubiquitous problems for conventional absorption chillers. In this research, a theoretical analysis and experimental investigation of an ultrathin hydrophobic film coated heat exchanger (UHFHE) with 1-Butyl-3-methylimidazolium chloride (BmimCl - Ionic Liquid) and Multiwalled Carbon Nanotubes/Propylene Glycol (MWCNT/PG - Nanofluid) as heat transfer fluid that works with an absorption chiller is proposed. An analytical model is developed for an absorption chiller with UHFHE, where ionic nanofluid is promulgated to improve heat and mass transfer characteristics. Approximate solutions are obtained from the abridged governing equations for the heat exchanger and then used to predict the performance of the chiller. In this study, surface hydrophilization, flow velocity enhancement, and chemical addition measures were adopted to solve the problem of heat transfer fluid fouling via weakening the adhesion force between the ionic liquid-based nanofluid (INF) and heat transfer surface (HTS). The dispersed MWCNT/PG is blended with BmimCl to enhance the stability of the nanofluid and decrease the fouling factor occurring in the heat exchanger using an ultrasonicator, followed by ultrasonic cracking with an ultrasonic cell disrupter system. The main objective of this study is to estimate the heat transfer enhancement and fouling factor of ionic nanofluid (MWCNT/PG + BmimCl) based UHFHE at different volume concentrations of nanoparticles under turbulent conditions flowing through the tubular heat exchanger of the proposed absorption chiller. The thermodynamic performance of this system is compared with that of conventional evaporative coolers and mechanical vapor compression chillers in terms of key operating parameters and either demineralized water or electricity consumption. The outcome of this research is going to improve the Coefficient of Performance (COP) of the chiller by at least 10% from its present state of the art of about 70% performance efficiency and a database would be generated for the design of efficient heat exchangers for absorption chillers in oil and gas industry. This study would also mitigate the current challenges of low performance and the high cost of available absorption chillers systems for PETRONAS natural gas processing plants and beyond.

Hydrothermal performance improvement of an inserted Double Pipe Heat Exchanger with Ionanofluid

August 2018

To meet the growing energy demand, double pipe heat exchangers (DPHEX), which are used in a variety of energy and thermal engineering applications, require improved heat transfer performance. In this study, a new class of surfactant-free Ionanofluid was formulated and utilized in an insert fitted DPHEX to improve the hydrothermal performance. A binary solution of [BMIM] [BF4] + deionized water was used as the base fluid and two-dimensional MXene nanoparticles at three different concentrations of 0.01, 0.05, and 0.10 wt.% were dispersed into the solution to formulate the ionanofluid. The conventional twisted tape (TT) insert is modified by introducing different types of geometrical (triangular, rectangular, and circular) cuts on its surface. The thermophysical (thermal conductivity, specific heat capacity, and density), and rheological (viscosity) properties are measured. A Zeta potential analysis was executed to assess the dispersion stability. A numerical solution was executed to investigate the combined effect of Ionanofluid and modified inserts on the laminar flow regime. According to the Ionanofluid characterization, 0.10 wt. % Ionanofluid had the highest thermal conductivity enhancement of 64% and heat capacity of 16.1% when compared to the base fluid with a maximum enhancement in Nu of about 116-136 % at varying Re. However, the increase in friction factor was substantial for this combination. Nevertheless, the value of PEC for the combination of Ionanofluid and RCTT insert, remaining between 1.61 and 1.68 over the entire range of Re, is greater than other Ionanofluid and insert combinations.
